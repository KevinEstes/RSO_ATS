Richmond Symphony

History

Founded in 1957, the Richmond Symphony is the largest performing arts organization in Central Virginia. The organization includes an orchestra of more than 70 professional musicians, the 150-voice Richmond Symphony Chorus and more than 260 students in the Richmond Symphony Youth Orchestra programs. Each season, more than 200,000 members of the community enjoy concerts, radio broadcasts, and educational outreach programs. The Richmond Symphony is partially funded by the Virginia Commission for the Arts and the National Endowment for the Arts.

Mission

The Richmond Symphony performs, teaches and champions music, to enrich and entertain communities throughout Virginia

Vacancy Details

Essential duties include performing in our 69 member orchestra and can include work in public schools. Other responsibilities are outlined in our Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA). Audition winners are expected to begin as soon as possible following an offer of employment during the upcomming season. Solo opportunities may also be possible. The first year of a musician’s employment is considered probationary. Employment terms and conditions will be detailed be the CBA, which is the definitive document regarding this position. The Richmond Symphony core receives two paid weeks off during the orchestra season and six paid personal services. Core member may accumulate up to 90 paid sick leave services, at the rate of two services for each month of employment, to use during illness, childbirth, or to care for an ill family member. Benefits presently include employer contributions toward medical insurance, parental leave, instrument insurance, long-term disability, and pension. Additionally, self-funded dental insurance, flex-spend account, and 403b retirement options are available.

Process

Those chosen to audition will receive an invitation by e-mail. We recommend that you not make travel plans or assume that you will attend the audition unless you have received an invitation. If you need to withdraw from the audition, please do so two weeks prior to your scheduled preliminary round. Late cancellations are very disruptive to the audition process. To cancel your audition please contact Kevin Estes – Personnel Manager | 804-387-7069 | kestes@richmondsymphony.com. The preliminary round will be screened and a proctor will introduce candidates by number. During the final round(s), the screen may or may not be removed. The audition committee reserves the right to dismiss anyone during any round should the candidate not meet the desired artistic level of performance. Finalists may be asked to perform with the orchestra before a winner is named. If the position is filled by an existing member of the orchestra, the resulting vacancy may be offered to a finalist on a temporary basis. The first year of a musician’s employment is considered probationary. Due to the prominence of this position, we do not allow auditions for comments only.

Eligibility Requirements

The Richmond Symphony is an equal opportunity employer. We employ highly qualified musicians who are authorized to work in the United States. Richmond Symphony employees are required to complete an I-9 and to provide corresponding documentation when they begin work, which could consist of a trial period. Failure to do so may result in the forfeiture of employment. The State of Virginia and Virginia Public Schools, with whom we work regularly, have regulations relevant to this position. Consequently, the Richmond Symphony does not employ those convicted of a felony, any offense involving the sexual molestation, or physical abuse of a child, or a crime of moral turpitude. All employees must confirm they meet these requirements. Employment offers are contingent a job-related criminal background check.
